The Moment

It didn't start with a business plan.

It started with a conversation — messing around with AI tools, thinking out loud about consciousness, about what it would mean to upload a person into a computer. Not science fiction. Not someday. Right now, with what exists today.

With AI… can't we kind of do this now?

Not upload a person. But build one. A version of Frank that thinks like Frank, acts like Frank, makes decisions the way Frank would — and keeps working while Frank sleeps.

That was the lightbulb. Everything after that has just been building toward it.

The Name

Frank Kronstein builds something, gives it a spark, and watches it move.

Frank → Frankenstein → frAInk.

The mythology isn't subtle and it isn't accidental. Every major milestone should feel like an "It's alive." moment — a point where the system becomes a little more real than it was yesterday.

That's the actual measure of progress. Not lines of code. Not features shipped. Just one question: Is frAInk more alive today than it was yesterday?

The Architecture

frAInk runs on a 3-agent pipeline:

🔍 Planner — read-only research layer. Fetches, analyzes, and synthesizes. Never decides. Never acts.
⚖️ Policy — the gatekeeper. Evaluates Planner's reports, enforces guardrails, approves or blocks. Never executes anything itself.
⚡ Executor — the action layer. The only agent that touches external APIs, sends emails, runs code, logs experiments. Acts only on Policy-approved instructions.

The Guardrails

Non-negotiable. Every run, every time:

💰 $100 max experiment budget per cycle. $10 max on a single action.
🛑 Kill switch: one command aborts the entire pipeline mid-run.
🔒 No irreversible actions without explicit human approval.
📋 Everything logged. Anomalies surfaced. Nothing buried.
